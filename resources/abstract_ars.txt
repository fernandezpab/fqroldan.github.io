Procyclical capital controls are a typical recommendation of sudden stops models, in which the government and the private sector share knowledge about the nature of shocks. This gives the government a clear understanding of the severity of the pecuniary externality and the extent of overborrowing, which is curbed by optimal policy. In a world with uncertainty about persistence, the currency of denomination of private contracts contains information about the private sector’s expectations. We explore the cyclicality of optimal capital controls when the government can condition on the composition of the private sector’s balance sheet.
